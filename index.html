Operational Margin Index (OMI) Tracking:
The slider gauge maps your real-time profile safety baseline. A perfect score places the alignment pin on the far left (Max Safety Margin). As cumulative profile risks mount, the engine pushes the pin to the right across the spectrum toward Critical Risk. The ready to dive percentage is inversely proportional to the accumulated risk score.

Rule of Thumb
The diver might consider not planning a dive to exceed the percentage of the max NDL for given depth that the Ready2Dive Index displays. For example, if the Ready2Dive Index is 68%, consider a bottom time that does not exceed 68% of the max NDL/min deco allowed time. A user could also consider setting GF High no higher than this percentage to ensure a dive-specific safety margin is applied in an attempt to mitigate risk.

Risk Threshold Rules (6–34 Max):

  • GREEN Status (6–9 Points): Optimal track. High operational safety margins. Situational parameters (3–6) can tolerate a single Amber selection and remain Green provided the cumulative score does not exceed 9 points.
  • AMBER Status (10–20 Points): Compromised safety margin window. Prompts added profile padding and conservative depth ceiling limits. *Note: Selecting Amber or Red in physiological parameters (1 or 2), or selecting Red in ANY category forces an automatic baseline transition to Amber regardless of total score.
  • RED Status (21–34 Points): Critical hazard accumulation. Safety margins severely depleted. Strongly prompts dive plan reassessment or cancellation.
  • Physiological Weighting (Parameters 1 & 2): Sleep & Circadian Disturbance (Category 1) and Hydration & Physical Recovery (Category 2) are heavily weighted at an accelerated scale (+4 for Amber, +7 for Red). This disproportionate value directly mirrors increased systemic vulnerability to Decompression Sickness (DCS) caused by acute exhaustion or vascular dehydration.
